More than a shelter, The Sacred Earth Goshala is a living testament to India's timeless bond with the bovine spirit.

History and Purpose

Founded on the ancestral grounds of the Krishna Valley, our Goshala began as a humble commitment to preserve the native breeds that have sustained Indian civilization for millennia.

What started with three rescued Desi cows has blossomed into a sanctuary housing over four hundred beings. Our purpose transcends mere protection; we aim to restore the ecological and spiritual equilibrium of the land through the revival of Vedic agriculture and cow-centric living.

Ethical Care

Every resident is treated as a family member. We practice 'Ahimsa' milking and provide lifetime shelter to non-milking cows and bulls, ensuring dignity in every stage of life.

Sustainability

Our ecosystem is a closed loop. We convert cow waste into organic fertilizers (Panchagavya) and clean energy, enriching the soil and reducing our carbon footprint.

self_improvement

Spirituality

The Goshala is a space for meditation and connection. We host satsangs and workshops that explore the spiritual significance of 'Go-Seva' in the modern world.

A Day in the Sanctuary

The rhythm of life at The Sacred Earth begins before dawn with the soft chanting of Vedic mantras. Our caretakers, many of whom have been with us for generations, know each cow by name and temperament. From the morning grazing in open meadows to the evening 'Aarti', every moment is an act of devotion.
